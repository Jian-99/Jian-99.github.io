Title: LiDAR-assisted Wi-Fi Heatmap Generation

Date: Sep 2020 - Present

Location: UBC, Vancouver

Team: Capstone Team 106

Key design objectives of subteam avionics

The ultimate task that fell upon us was to develop a comprehensive data aquisition system that demostrates the key parameters of our designed aircraft while it's in the air. The work was divided into two main sections with one on the software side and another being related to hardwares. Although my personal focus was mainly on the latter, the final product of our ground station can be visualized by the following picture.

In order to deliver the aquired parameters accurately, we chose Arduino as the platform where we intergrated all sensors to simultaneously extract the values of altitude, orientation, location, and so on.

That being said, such work of integration might not be ealiy achieved. Owing to certain specifications of our sensors in use, we ended up running multiple Arduino timers to make sure the different hardware pieces work perfectly in parallel. The final connection scheme is shown below.

Another picture above is me using UBC's wind tunnel to calibrate MPXV7002 which senses pressure from the mechincal structure of the pitot tube.

Title: Reluctance Motor with PID Control*

Date: Jan- Mar, 2020

Location: UBC, Vancouver

Team Members:

Jian Gao, Jacky Yu
Matthew Lee, Hao Luo

Supervisor: Dr. Leo Stocco

Electrical Engineering Design Studio II*

Before UBC campus was shut down due to the concern of COVID-19, the original objective of the project was to build a robot arm that plays a musical instrument upon a team's preference. Detailed requirements are listed as:

  1. 4-bar linkage robot mechanism
  2. 2 identical motors
  3. All necessary sensors & drive electronics
  4. Control system
  5. Simulated electro-mechanical system using Simulink & SimulationX

However, since the project couldn't be continued as we wished (All classed were shifted to be delivered in an online format), this course ended up with evaluating what we had achieved in first two demos. As for my team, we had completed the design of the gear system and the assemble of a motor from watercut metal pieces with a customized 3D-printed motor case. More details are revealed in the video demos and the full report below.
